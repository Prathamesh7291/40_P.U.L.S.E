What is the need of healthy diet ?

Those who think they have no time for healthy eating will sooner or later for healthy eating will sooner or later have to find time for illness.

Although you might know that eating certain foods can increase your heart disease risk, changing your eating habits is often tough. Once you know which foods to eat more of and which foods to limit, you"ll be on your way toward a heart-healthy diet.

Fruits and Veggies

Include

  • Fresh and Frozen vegetables
  • Low-sodium canned vegetables
  • Canned fruit packed in juice or water

Avoid

  • Coconut
  • Vegetables with creamy sauces
  • Fried or breaded vegetables
  • Canned fruit packed in heavy syrup

Whole Grains

Include

  • Whole-wheat flour
  • Whole-grain pasta
  • Oatmeal (steel-cut or regular)

Avoid

  • Muffins
  • Biscuits
  • Frozen waffles
  • Buttered popcorn

Unhealthy Fats

Include

  • Olive oil
  • Canola oil
  • Margarine, trans fat free
  • Nuts, seeds

Avoid

  • Butter
  • Cream sauce
  • Bacon fat
  • Lard

Low-fat protein sources:

Include

  • Eggs
  • Legumes
  • Lean ground meats

Avoid

  • Spareribs
  • Hot dogs and sausages
  • Bacon
  • Fatty and marbled meats

Limit or reduce salt:

Include

  • Herbs and spices
  • Salt-free seasoning blends
  • Ketchup

Avoid

  • Table salt
  • Restaurant meals
  • Canned soups and prepared foods,
